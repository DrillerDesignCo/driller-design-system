### H2: What Goes in a Construction Dumpster

Our roll-off dumpsters accept most common construction and demolition debris, including:

- Roofing shingles and underlayment
- Drywall and plaster
- Lumber, plywood, and framing scraps
- Concrete, brick, and block (weight limits apply)
- Metal — rebar, flashing, ductwork, siding
- Flooring — tile, carpet, vinyl, hardwood
- Windows, doors, and fixtures
- Landscaping debris from site prep

**What we can't take:** Hazardous materials, chemicals, paint, asbestos, and refrigerants. If you're not sure about an item, call us and we'll let you know. For items we can't accept, our [Brazos Valley Disposal Guide](/resources/disposal-guide/) shows you where to take them locally.

### H2: Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I put concrete in a construction dumpster?**
Yes. We accept concrete, brick, block, and similar heavy materials. Weight limits apply — call us to confirm the weight allowance for your container size so you can plan your loads.

**How heavy can a construction dumpster get?**
Each size has a set weight limit included in your flat-rate quote. If you're hauling heavy debris like concrete or roofing shingles, we'll help you pick the right size so you stay within the limit and avoid overage fees.

**Do you offer same-day construction dumpster delivery?**
We often deliver the same day you call, depending on availability. For scheduled job sites, we recommend booking a day or two ahead so we can guarantee your preferred drop-off time.

**Can I get multiple dumpsters on one job site?**
Yes. We regularly place two or more containers on active construction sites. Call us to set up a multi-container plan with a delivery and swap schedule that matches your project phases.

**What if I need a dumpster swapped, not picked up?**
Just call us when the container is full. We'll pick it up and drop a fresh one — often the same day. This keeps your site clean without gaps in service.
